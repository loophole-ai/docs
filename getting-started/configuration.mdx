Loophole works out of the box, but you'll get the most out of it once you plug in your own AI keys. This page walks you through that.

## Add your AI provider

Loophole supports a bunch of AI providers. You only need one to get going.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open settings">
    Press `Cmd+,` (Mac) or `Ctrl+,` (Windows/Linux) to open Settings.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Go to the AI tab">
    On the left sidebar of settings, click **AI Providers**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Paste your key">
    Pick a provider and paste your API key in the box.

    - **OpenAI** — get a key at [platform.openai.com](https://platform.openai.com/api-keys)
    - **Anthropic** — get a key at [console.anthropic.com](https://console.anthropic.com/)
    - **Google Gemini** — get a key at [aistudio.google.com](https://aistudio.google.com/)
    - **Local models** — point Loophole at your Ollama or LM Studio instance
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pick your default model">
    Choose which model to use by default. You can always switch later from the chat panel.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Use a local model

Want to run everything on your own machine? Loophole works with [Ollama](https://ollama.com/) and [LM Studio](https://lmstudio.ai/).

1. Install Ollama and pull a model (for example, `ollama pull qwen2.5-coder`).
2. In Loophole, go to **Settings → AI Providers → Local**.
3. Set the URL to `http://localhost:11434` and pick your model.

## Other settings worth tweaking

- **Theme** — light, dark, or system
- **Font size** — make the editor easier on your eyes
- **Keybindings** — switch to VS Code, Vim, or Emacs style
- **Autosave** — turn it on if you forget to hit save

## Where settings live

Your settings are stored on your computer at:

- **Mac:** `~/Library/Application Support/Loophole/settings.json`
- **Windows:** `%APPDATA%\Loophole\settings.json`
- **Linux:** `~/.config/Loophole/settings.json`

You can edit that file by hand if you want.
