---
title: "Code of conduct"
description: "How we treat each other in the Loophole community"
---

We want Loophole to be a place where anyone can pitch in, ask questions, and build cool things — no matter who they are.

## The basics

Be kind. Assume good intent. Help people out when you can.

That's the whole thing, honestly. But if you want more detail, read on.

## What's welcome

- Asking questions, even "dumb" ones
- Disagreeing with ideas (not people)
- Sharing your work
- Helping new contributors get started
- Giving feedback that's specific and useful

## What's not

- Personal attacks or insults
- Harassment of any kind
- Discrimination based on race, gender, sexuality, religion, disability, or anything else
- Posting other people's private info
- Spam or self-promotion that doesn't help anyone
- Being a jerk "as a joke"

## Where this applies

Everywhere Loophole has a presence:

- The GitHub repo (issues, PRs, discussions)
- Our Discord server
- Our social media accounts
- Any official Loophole events

## If something goes wrong

If someone's breaking these rules, or you see something that's not okay, email us at [conduct@loophole.dev](mailto:conduct@loophole.dev). We'll take it seriously and we'll keep you anonymous if you ask.

We might:

- Talk to the person and ask them to stop
- Remove their comments or posts
- Ban them from the community for a while or forever

We'd rather not have to do any of this. Just be cool to each other.

## A note from us

This stuff isn't about being uptight — it's about making sure everyone feels welcome to contribute. Some of the best PRs we've ever gotten came from first-time open source contributors. We want more of that.
